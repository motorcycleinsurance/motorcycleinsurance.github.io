Cheap Car Insurance for Classic Cars: What You Need to Know

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Classic cars aren’t just vehicles—they’re pieces of history, passion projects, and sometimes even lucrative investments. But insuring them doesn’t have to break the bank. Whether you own a vintage Mustang, a rare Porsche, or a lovingly restored Chevy, finding affordable insurance is possible if you know where to look and what factors influence your premiums.

Why Classic Car Insurance Differs from Standard Policies

Unlike everyday vehicles, classic cars are typically driven less frequently, maintained meticulously, and often stored in secure locations. Because of these differences, insurers offer specialized policies tailored to the unique needs of classic car owners.

Key Differences:

  • Agreed Value Coverage: Standard auto insurance pays out based on actual cash value (depreciated), but classic car policies often use an agreed-upon value determined upfront.
  • Limited Mileage: Many classic car insurers offer lower rates if you drive fewer than a certain number of miles annually (e.g., 2,500–5,000 miles).
  • Storage Requirements: Discounts may apply if your car is stored in a locked garage or climate-controlled facility.

How to Find Affordable Classic Car Insurance

1. Choose the Right Provider

Not all insurers specialize in classic cars. Companies like Hagerty, Grundy, and American Modern focus exclusively on collector vehicles and often provide better rates than traditional insurers.

2. Bundle Policies

If you already have home or auto insurance, ask your provider about bundling discounts for adding a classic car policy. Some companies offer multi-vehicle discounts for collectors with multiple classics.

3. Increase Your Deductible

Opting for a higher deductible can lower your monthly premiums—just make sure you can afford the out-of-pocket cost if you need to file a claim.

4. Join a Car Club

Membership in organizations like the Antique Automobile Club of America (AACA) or the Classic Car Club of America (CCCA) can qualify you for exclusive insurance discounts.

5. Maintain a Clean Driving Record

Insurers reward safe drivers. Avoid tickets and accidents to keep your rates low.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Underinsuring Your Classic

Agreed value coverage is crucial—don’t settle for a policy that undervalues your car’s true worth.

Ignoring Usage Restrictions

If your policy includes mileage limits, exceeding them could void your coverage. Track your driving habits carefully.

Skipping Documentation

Keep receipts, appraisals, and photos of your car’s condition. Proper documentation ensures smoother claims processing.

The Impact of Global Trends on Classic Car Insurance

Rising Demand for Classics

As electric vehicles (EVs) dominate headlines, interest in gas-powered classics has surged. Limited supply and growing demand are driving up values—making proper insurance even more critical.

Climate Change and Storage Risks

Extreme weather events (floods, wildfires) pose new risks to classic cars. Insurers now factor in geographic risks when setting premiums.

The Role of Technology

GPS trackers and advanced security systems can lower premiums by reducing theft risk. Some insurers even offer discounts for cars equipped with these features.

Final Thoughts

Owning a classic car is a rewarding experience, but protecting it shouldn’t be a financial burden. By understanding policy options, leveraging discounts, and staying informed about industry trends, you can secure affordable coverage without compromising on protection. Whether you’re a weekend cruiser or a serious collector, the right insurance ensures your prized possession stays on the road—and out of financial jeopardy—for years to come.

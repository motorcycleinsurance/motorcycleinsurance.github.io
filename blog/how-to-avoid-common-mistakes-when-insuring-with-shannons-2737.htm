How to Avoid Common Mistakes When Insuring with Shannons

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

When it comes to insuring your prized possessions—whether it’s a classic car, a rare motorcycle, or a collection of vintage memorabilia—Shannons Insurance is a name that often comes up. Known for its specialized coverage and deep understanding of enthusiast markets, Shannons offers policies tailored to unique needs. However, even with a reputable provider, policyholders can make costly mistakes. In today’s fast-changing world, where inflation, climate risks, and digital fraud are on the rise, avoiding these pitfalls is more critical than ever.

Understanding Shannons Insurance

Shannons is not your average insurer. It caters specifically to collectors, restorers, and enthusiasts, offering agreed-value policies that reflect the true worth of rare or customized items. Unlike market-value policies, which can leave you underinsured, Shannons ensures you get what your asset is actually worth in case of a total loss.

Why People Choose Shannons

  • Agreed-Value Coverage: No haggling over depreciation.
  • Flexible Usage Terms: Unlike standard insurers, Shannons often allows for limited competitive use or club events.
  • Expert Appraisals: Their team understands niche markets, ensuring accurate valuations.

Despite these advantages, policyholders sometimes stumble into avoidable errors.

Common Mistakes and How to Dodge Them

1. Underestimating the True Value of Your Asset

One of the biggest blunders is undervaluing your insured item. With inflation driving up restoration costs and parts scarcity (thanks to global supply chain disruptions), a policy based on outdated valuations can leave you shortchanged.

How to Avoid It:
- Get regular professional appraisals, especially after modifications.
- Document upgrades with receipts and photos.
- Consider "inflation guard" riders if available.

2. Ignoring Climate-Related Risks

Wildfires, floods, and extreme weather are becoming more frequent. If you store your classic car in a flood-prone garage or a wildfire-risk zone, standard coverage might not suffice.

How to Avoid It:
- Review Shannons’ policy exclusions for natural disasters.
- Invest in climate-proof storage (e.g., elevated garages, fire-resistant materials).
- Ask about add-ons like "named storm" coverage.

3. Overlooking Usage Restrictions

Shannons policies often come with mileage caps or usage clauses (e.g., no daily commuting). Violating these can void your claim.

How to Avoid It:
- Read the fine print on "approved use."
- If your habits change (e.g., you start using the car for weekend rallies), update your policy.
- Consider a higher-tier plan if you need more flexibility.

4. Skipping Documentation

In the digital age, failing to digitize records is a risk. A stolen car with no photos or receipts makes claim validation a nightmare.

How to Avoid It:
- Create a digital inventory with timestamps (use cloud storage).
- Keep hard copies of appraisals in a fireproof safe.
- Record videos of the asset’s condition annually.

5. Assuming "Agreed Value" Means No Questions Asked

Agreed value doesn’t mean automatic payouts. Insurers can still investigate fraud or misrepresentation.

How to Avoid It:
- Be transparent about modifications or damage history.
- Never exaggerate the asset’s value—it could backfire.

6. Neglecting Cybersecurity Risks

Cybercrime targeting insurers is rising. Phishing scams pretending to be Shannons could trick you into sharing policy details.

How to Avoid It:
- Verify emails/calls by contacting Shannons directly.
- Use two-factor authentication for online accounts.

Final Tips for a Smooth Experience

  • Bundle Policies: Shannons often discounts multi-asset coverage (e.g., cars + memorabilia).
  • Review Annually: Market values and personal circumstances change.
  • Build a Relationship: Specialist insurers like Shannons value long-term clients—ask about loyalty perks.

By sidestepping these traps, you’ll ensure your passion projects stay protected, no matter what the world throws your way.

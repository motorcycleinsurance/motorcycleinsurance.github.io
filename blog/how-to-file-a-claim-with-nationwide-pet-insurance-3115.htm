How to File a Claim with Nationwide Pet Insurance

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Pets are more than just animals—they’re family. In today’s world, where veterinary costs are skyrocketing due to inflation and advancements in pet healthcare, having pet insurance is no longer a luxury but a necessity. Nationwide Pet Insurance, one of the most trusted providers in the U.S., offers comprehensive coverage to ensure your furry friend gets the best care without breaking the bank. But how do you actually file a claim? Let’s break it down step by step.

Why Pet Insurance Matters in 2024

Rising Veterinary Costs

The cost of veterinary care has increased by nearly 40% over the past decade. From emergency surgeries to routine check-ups, pet parents are feeling the financial strain. With inflation affecting everything from pet food to medications, insurance helps mitigate unexpected expenses.

Increased Pet Ownership Post-Pandemic

The pandemic led to a surge in pet adoptions, with millions of families welcoming new furry members. As these pets age, the need for healthcare grows, making insurance a smart investment.

Advancements in Veterinary Medicine

From cancer treatments to orthopedic surgeries, veterinary medicine now offers life-saving procedures that were once unavailable. While this is great for pets, it also means higher bills—another reason why insurance is crucial.

Understanding Nationwide Pet Insurance Coverage

Before filing a claim, it’s important to know what your policy covers. Nationwide offers several plans, including:

Whole Pet with Wellness

This is the most comprehensive plan, covering accidents, illnesses, and routine care like vaccinations and dental cleanings.

Major Medical

This plan focuses on accidents and illnesses but excludes wellness care. It’s a good option for pet parents who want coverage for emergencies.

Pet Wellness

A standalone plan that covers only routine care, such as flea prevention and annual exams.

Each plan has different deductibles, reimbursement rates, and annual limits, so review your policy details carefully.

Step-by-Step Guide to Filing a Claim

Step 1: Visit Your Veterinarian

If your pet needs medical attention, take them to a licensed veterinarian. Nationwide covers any licensed vet in the U.S., including specialists and emergency clinics.

Step 2: Pay for the Services

Unlike human health insurance, pet insurance typically requires you to pay upfront and then file for reimbursement. Keep all itemized receipts and medical records—these are crucial for your claim.

Step 3: Gather Required Documents

To file a claim, you’ll need:
- A completed Nationwide claim form (available online)
- Itemized invoice from your vet
- Medical records related to the visit
- Proof of payment (receipt or credit card statement)

Step 4: Submit Your Claim

You can submit your claim in three ways:

Online Portal

Log in to your Nationwide account and upload the documents. This is the fastest method, with claims often processed within 10-14 business days.

Mobile App

Nationwide’s app allows you to snap photos of your receipts and submit them instantly.

Mail

If you prefer traditional methods, mail your documents to:
Nationwide Pet Insurance Claims
P.O. Box 2344
Brea, CA 92822

Step 5: Track Your Claim Status

After submission, you can check your claim status online or via the app. Nationwide will notify you via email once your claim is processed.

Common Reasons for Claim Denials (And How to Avoid Them)

Even with the best intentions, claims can get denied. Here’s why—and how to prevent it:

Pre-Existing Conditions

Nationwide, like most insurers, doesn’t cover pre-existing conditions. If your pet had an illness before enrollment, treatments for that condition won’t be reimbursed.

Missing Documentation

Forgetting to include an itemized invoice or medical records is a common mistake. Double-check before submitting!

Waiting Periods

Most policies have a 14-day waiting period for illnesses and a 48-hour period for accidents. Claims filed during this time will be denied.

Non-Covered Services

Cosmetic procedures (like tail docking) or breeding-related expenses aren’t covered. Always review your policy’s exclusions.

Tips for a Smooth Claims Process

Keep Digital Copies

Scan or photograph all vet documents and store them in a cloud folder. This ensures you never lose them.

Submit Claims Promptly

Nationwide allows up to 270 days from the treatment date to file a claim, but the sooner you submit, the faster you’ll get reimbursed.

Communicate with Your Vet

Ask your vet to provide detailed notes—insurance companies often request additional information if the initial records are unclear.

The Future of Pet Insurance

As pet healthcare evolves, so does insurance. Here are some trends to watch:

Telemedicine for Pets

Many insurers, including Nationwide, now cover virtual vet visits—a game-changer for minor issues.

Personalized Plans

AI-driven underwriting could soon offer customized policies based on your pet’s breed, age, and health history.

Expanded Coverage

Expect to see more policies covering alternative therapies like acupuncture and hydrotherapy.

Filing a claim with Nationwide Pet Insurance doesn’t have to be stressful. By understanding your policy, keeping organized records, and following these steps, you can ensure your pet gets the care they deserve—without the financial worry.
